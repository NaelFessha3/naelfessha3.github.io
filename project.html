Discovery Project Idea ‚Äì Starting Pitch

For my Discovery Project, I wanted to build something practical that also helped me learn real electrical and computer engineering skills. My initial idea was to create a temperature-controlled fan system that could automatically adjust its speed based on surrounding temperature.

Project Progress

I began by selecting all required components for the system: an Arduino Uno, DHT11 temperature sensor, 16x2 LCD, L298N motor driver, and a 5V DC fan. I first verified the sensor by reading temperature in the Serial Monitor, then displayed it on the LCD. After wiring the motor driver, I programmed the fan to change speeds based on temperature ranges. Finally, I combined all subsystems into one working unit.

Project Successes and Failures

During development I experienced several challenges, such as the LCD dimming when the fan activated due to voltage drops, and larger fans drawing too much current for the Arduino. I solved these by improving power management and using smaller fans with lower current demands. Despite these issues, I had multiple successes including accurate temperature readings, responsive fan control, and a fully functioning integrated system.

ECE Skills Gained

  • Microcontroller programming using Arduino C/C++
  • Sensor integration with the DHT11
  • Circuit prototyping and breadboard wiring
  • PWM-based motor speed control
  • Debugging hardware and software interactions
  • Understanding power supply requirements and driver limitations

Final Thoughts

This project increased my interest in embedded systems and reinforced my understanding of electrical engineering concepts. It helped me gain hands-on knowledge of sensors, displays, motor drivers, and microcontroller programming. In the future, I hope to expand this project by adding humidity-based control, a better driver, and possibly a Bluetooth/mobile interface for remote monitoring.
