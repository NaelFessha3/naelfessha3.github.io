Career Goals

I'm pursuing a career as an electrical engineer in the semiconductor industry, focused on analog and mixed-signal integrated circuit design. My academic focus at Georgia Tech is the Circuits & Electronic Devices thread, where I study the physics and design principles that bring transistors, sensors, and analog systems to life. I'm drawn to this field because semiconductor design sits at the intersection of physics, mathematics, and creative engineerin, every chip is an exercise in translating real-world signals into reliable, manufacturable hardware.

Long-term, I want to contribute to the design of analog front-ends, sensor interfaces, power management ICs, or RF circuits that enable next-generation communication, sensing, and energy-efficient electronics.

My Roadmap

Near-term (2026)

  • Complete core coursework in semiconductor device physics, analog circuits, and microelectronic fabrication
  • Build hands-on experience with SPICE simulation, schematic capture, and circuit prototyping using lab instrumentation
  • Pursue a summer internship or co-op in analog IC design, device engineering, or semiconductor process development

Mid-term (2026–2028)

  • Deepen expertise in CMOS analog design, layout, and verification
  • Take on advanced coursework in VLSI, RF design, and integrated sensors
  • Contribute to a research project or capstone involving full custom IC design
  • Build proficiency in industry tools such as Cadence Virtuoso and Synopsys HSPICE

Long-term (Post-graduation)

  • Begin my career as an analog or mixed-signal design engineer at a leading semiconductor company
  • Pursue a Master's or PhD in Electrical Engineering focused on integrated circuit design or device engineering
  • Grow into a senior IC design role contributing to high-performance, energy-efficient analog and mixed-signal systems
